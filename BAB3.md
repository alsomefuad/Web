## BAB 3: MANAJEMEN KINERJA BERBASIS OKR (OBJECTIVES & KEY RESULTS)

### 3.1 Pengantar: Mengapa Organisasi Mahasiswa Butuh OKR?

Pernahkah Anda mengalami situasi ini?

**Scenario 1: Busy Tapi Tidak Jelas**
```
Organisasi kalian super sibuk. Rapat seminggu 3x. Acara hampir tiap bulan. Semua orang 
exhausted.

Tapi kalau ditanya: "Apa achievement kita tahun ini?" ‚Üí Bingung jawabnya.
"Kita berhasil mencapai apa?" ‚Üí Tidak jelas.
"Organisasi kita sekarang lebih baik dari tahun lalu?" ‚Üí Tidak bisa diukur.
```

**Scenario 2: Acara Banyak, Impact Minimal**
```
Tahun ini ada 10 acara. Tapi setelah acara, tidak ada yang berubah. Participants 
datang, terus done. Tidak ada long-term impact atau behavioral change.

Organisasi exist untuk exist. Tidak ada tujuan yang lebih besar.
```

**Scenario 3: Tidak Aligned**
```
Divisi A fokus ke kuantitas acara: "Kita harus ada acara tiap bulan!"
Divisi B fokus ke kualitas member: "Kita harus intense training anggota!"
Divisi C fokus ke publikasi: "Instagram followers harus naik!"

Semua busy dengan agenda masing-masing, tapi tidak ada coherent direction. End of year: 
Capek tapi fragmentated.
```

Sound familiar? Ini adalah problem dari **tidak ada sistem manajemen kinerja yang jelas**.

Organisasi tradisional biasanya pakai **KPI (Key Performance Indicators)**:
- "Harus ada minimal 8 acara per tahun"
- "Partisipan minimal 500 orang total"
- "Instagram followers harus 2000"

Masalahnya dengan KPI tradisional:
- **Too rigid**: Circumstances bisa berubah, tapi KPI stuck
- **Quantity over quality**: Fokus ke angka, bukan impact
- **Not inspiring**: Anggota tidak feel connected ke numbers
- **Blame culture**: Kalau miss target, cari siapa yang salah

Enter **OKR (Objectives and Key Results)**.

### 3.2 Apa Itu OKR?

**OKR = Objectives and Key Results**

Sistem ini dipopulerkan oleh Google dan sekarang digunakan oleh ribuan organisasi worldwide, dari startup sampai Fortune 500 companies.

**Simple definition:**
- **Objective (O)**: WHERE you want to go (qualitative, inspirational)
- **Key Results (KRs)**: HOW you know you're getting there (quantitative, measurable)

**Formula:**
```
I will [Objective]
as measured by [Key Result 1, Key Result 2, Key Result 3]
```

**Contoh simple:**

**Bad (tidak jelas):**
"Kita mau jadi organisasi yang lebih baik"

**Good (OKR):**
```
Objective: Menjadikan organisasi sebagai wadah #1 untuk pengembangan soft skills di kampus

Key Results:
1. 80% anggota mengikuti minimal 2 workshop soft skills per semester
2. Tingkat kepuasan anggota terhadap program development mencapai 4.5/5.0
3. 90% alumni yang survey report bahwa skills dari organisasi membantu karir mereka
```

**Lihat perbedaannya?**

| Traditional Goal | OKR |
|---|---|
| "Jadi lebih baik" (vague) | "Jadi wadah #1 untuk soft skills" (specific, ambitious) |
| Tidak ada cara ukur | 3 Key Results yang jelas dan measurable |
| Tidak inspiring | Connect dengan purpose yang bigger |
| Sulit track progress | Bisa track per KR |

### 3.3 Karakteristik Good OKR

Tidak semua OKR dibuat sama. Ada OKR yang powerful dan ada yang justru counterproductive. Mari kita pahami karakteristik OKR yang baik.

#### A. CHARACTERISTICS OF GOOD OBJECTIVES

**1. Qualitative & Inspirational**

Objective harus membangkitkan semangat, bukan sekadar deskripsi tugas.

‚ùå **Bad Objective:**
"Melaksanakan kegiatan organisasi"
- Generic, tidak ada soul
- Bisa apply ke organisasi manapun
- Tidak ada aspiration

‚úÖ **Good Objective:**
"Menjadikan organisasi sebagai rumah kedua bagi mahasiswa untuk berkembang dan berkarya"
- Emotional connection
- Paint a picture
- Meaningful

**2. Action-Oriented**

Objective harus clear tentang apa yang ingin dicapai, menggunakan action verbs.

‚ùå **Bad:**
"Meningkatkan kualitas"
- Kualitas apa?
- Increase dari apa ke apa?

‚úÖ **Good:**
"Membangun reputasi sebagai organisasi paling inovatif di kampus"
- Clear action: membangun
- Clear target: reputasi sebagai paling inovatif
- Clear scope: di kampus

**Powerful Action Verbs untuk Objectives:**
- Membangun (Build)
- Menjadi (Become)
- Menciptakan (Create)
- Mentransformasi (Transform)
- Memperluas (Expand)
- Mengembangkan (Develop)
- Memimpin (Lead)
- Menguasai (Master)

**3. Time-Bound (Implicitly)**

Objective harus untuk periode waktu specific (biasanya quarter atau semester).

‚ùå **Bad:**
"Menjadi organisasi terbaik" (kapan?)

‚úÖ **Good:**
"Menjadi organisasi paling aktif di fakultas **semester ini**"
- Clear timeframe
- Creates urgency
- Measurable dalam periode defined

**4. Ambitious but Achievable**

Sweet spot: 70-80% confidence bisa achieve.

**The Scale:**
```
0-50% chance: Moonshot (terlalu ambitious, likely to demoralize)
50-70% chance: Stretch goal (ideal OKR zone)
70-90% chance: Challenging but doable
90-100% chance: Too easy, tidak push boundaries
```

‚ùå **Too Easy:**
"Mengadakan 1 acara semester ini"
(Organisasi pasti bisa, tidak ada challenge)

‚úÖ **Stretch Goal:**
"Mengadakan 5 acara high-impact dengan minimal 200 peserta per acara"
(Challenging tapi possible dengan effort)

‚ùå **Too Ambitious:**
"Menjadi organisasi mahasiswa terbaik se-Indonesia"
(Unless Anda sudah top-tier, ini unrealistic untuk semester pertama)

**5. Aligned dengan Vision/Mission**

Objective harus ladder up ke organizational purpose.

**Misalnya organisasi dengan mission:**
"Mengembangkan kepemimpinan dan profesionalisme mahasiswa"

‚úÖ **Aligned Objective:**
"Membekali 100% anggota dengan leadership skills yang applicable di dunia kerja"
(Direct support mission)

‚ùå **Misaligned Objective:**
"Menjadi organisasi dengan Instagram followers terbanyak"
(Bisa jadi means, tapi bukan end yang aligned dengan mission)

#### B. CHARACTERISTICS OF GOOD KEY RESULTS

**1. Quantitative & Measurable**

Key Result HARUS ada angka atau metric yang jelas.

‚ùå **Bad Key Result:**
"Meningkatkan kepuasan anggota"
- "Meningkatkan" seberapa?
- "Kepuasan" diukur bagaimana?

‚úÖ **Good Key Result:**
"Tingkat kepuasan anggota mencapai 4.3/5.0 berdasarkan end-of-semester survey"
- Clear metric: 4.3/5.0
- Clear measurement method: survey
- Clear timing: end-of-semester

**Key Result Formula:**
```
[Action Verb] + [Metric] + [From X to Y] or [Reach Z] + [by When]
```

**Examples:**
- "Increase Instagram followers dari 2,000 ke 3,500 by akhir semester"
- "Achieve event attendance rate of 85% across all programs"
- "Reduce operational costs by 20% compared to last year"
- "Obtain 3 new corporate sponsors dengan total funding minimal Rp 15 juta"

**2. Outcome-Focused, Not Activity-Focused**

Key Result mengukur HASIL, bukan aktivitas.

‚ùå **Activity-based (Bad):**
"Mengadakan 8 workshop"
- Ini adalah to-do list, bukan key result
- Tidak guarantee impact apapun
- Bisa conduct 8 workshop tapi attendance 0%

‚úÖ **Outcome-based (Good):**
"80% anggota mengikuti minimal 2 workshop dengan satisfaction score 4.5/5.0"
- Mengukur participation dan quality
- Focus pada impact, bukan hanya execution

**How to convert Activity to Outcome:**

| Activity | Why doing this? | Outcome-based KR |
|----------|----------------|------------------|
| Mengadakan workshop | Agar anggota develop skills | 80% anggota report skill improvement |
| Posting di Instagram | Agar reach lebih luas | Instagram reach 10K per week dengan engagement 6% |
| Membuat SOP | Agar operasional lebih smooth | Operational errors berkurang 50% |
| Rekrutmen anggota | Agar organisasi grow | Active members naik dari 50 ke 80 |

**3. Verifiable & Objective**

Dua orang berbeda harus bisa lihat data yang sama dan reach kesimpulan yang sama.

‚ùå **Subjective:**
"Organisasi menjadi lebih dikenal"
- Siapa yang define "lebih dikenal"?
- Based on feeling atau fakta?

‚úÖ **Verifiable:**
"Brand awareness meningkat: 60% mahasiswa fakultas recognize logo organisasi (via survey)"
- Clear method: survey
- Clear percentage: 60%
- Siapa saja yang lihat survey results akan tahu apakah achieved atau tidak

**4. Relevant to the Objective**

Setiap Key Result harus directly contribute to achieving the Objective.

**Test of Relevance:**
Tanya: "Kalau semua Key Results ini achieved, apakah Objective otomatis achieved atau at least significantly progressed?"

**Example:**

**Objective:** Menjadi organisasi paling impactful di kampus

**Key Results:**
‚úÖ "500+ mahasiswa participate dalam programs kami (relevance: more participation = more impact)"
‚úÖ "85% participants report actionable learning (relevance: quality impact)"
‚úÖ "3 kampanye sosial dengan measurable community outcome (relevance: tangible impact)"

‚ùå "Instagram followers naik 200%"
- Ini bisa relevant untuk awareness, tapi tidak directly measure impact
- Better: "Instagram content generates 50+ meaningful conversations tentang social issues"

**5. Limited in Number (3-5 per Objective)**

Terlalu banyak Key Results = diluted focus.

**The Magic Number: 3-5**

‚ùå **Too Few (1-2):**
"Objective: Meningkatkan kualitas program
KR 1: Survey satisfaction 4.5/5.0"
- Too narrow, banyak aspek quality yang tidak captured

‚ùå **Too Many (8+):**
"Objective: Meningkatkan engagement
KR 1: Attendance
KR 2: Survey score
KR 3: Social media interaction
KR 4: Volunteer hours
KR 5: Retention rate
KR 6: Referral rate
KR 7: Event participation
KR 8: Active contribution"
- Overwhelming, tidak bisa focus
- Sulit track dan manage

‚úÖ **Just Right (3-5):**
"Objective: Meningkatkan engagement
KR 1: 85% attendance rate di activities
KR 2: Retention rate 80% (anggota stay active full semester)
KR 3: 70% members actively contribute (bukan hanya passive participants)
KR 4: NPS (Net Promoter Score) minimal 50"
- Comprehensive tapi manageable
- Cover different dimensions of engagement
- Actionable

#### C. COMMON OKR MISTAKES & HOW TO FIX

**Mistake 1: Sandbagging (Setting Targets Too Low)**

**Why it happens:**
Fear of failure atau pressure to always meet targets

**Example:**
Organisasi historically bisa get 300 participants per event.
Sandbag OKR: "Get 100 participants per event"
‚Üí Achieved 100%... but didn't push themselves

**Fix:**
‚úÖ Embrace 70-80% achievement as success
‚úÖ "If you consistently hit 100%, your targets are too low"
‚úÖ Better OKR: "Get 400+ participants per event"
   - Even kalau achieve 350 (87.5%), that's GREAT and you pushed boundaries

**Mistake 2: Confusing OKRs with To-Do Lists**

**Example:**
‚ùå "O: Complete semester programs
    KR1: Conduct meeting every week
    KR2: Create Instagram content
    KR3: Submit reports on time"

This is task list, bukan OKR.

**Fix:**
‚úÖ "O: Build highly engaged community
    KR1: 80% meeting attendance dengan avg satisfaction 4.5/5
    KR2: Instagram content generates avg 200 meaningful interactions per post
    KR3: 100% reports submitted with actionable insights adopted"

See? Same activities, tapi framed as outcomes.

**Mistake 3: Not Updating or Tracking**

OKR yang dibuat terus ditinggalkan, baru diingat pas akhir semester.

**Fix:**
‚úÖ Weekly check-ins (15-30 min)
‚úÖ Monthly reviews
‚úÖ Visible dashboard (Notion board, whiteboard, Trello)
‚úÖ Integrate OKR discussion dalam regular meetings

**Mistake 4: Individual vs Team OKRs Tidak Aligned**

Setiap orang punya OKR sendiri yang tidak support team OKR.

**Example misalignment:**
- Team OKR: "Improve member retention"
- Individual OKR: "Maximize event quantity"
‚Üí Conflict: Too many events bisa overwhelm members dan hurt retention

**Fix:**
‚úÖ Cascade OKRs: Team ‚Üí Division ‚Üí Individual
‚úÖ Ensure alignment: "My OKR should contribute to team OKR"

**Mistake 5: Treating OKR as Performance Review Tool**

Using OKR untuk reward/punishment.

**Problem:**
- People will sandbag
- Risk-aversion
- Gaming the metrics
- Dishonest reporting

**Fix:**
‚úÖ OKR adalah learning tool, bukan evaluation tool
‚úÖ Separate OKRs from formal performance reviews
‚úÖ Celebrate learning dari missed OKRs
‚úÖ Culture message: "We set ambitious goals. Not achieving 100% adalah expected dan okay."

#### D. SELF-CHECK: Is Your OKR Good?

**OBJECTIVE Checklist:**

Before finalizing your Objective, tanya:
- [ ] Is it inspiring? (Do people get excited about this?)
- [ ] Is it clear? (Everyone understand apa yang meant?)
- [ ] Is it ambitious? (Does it push us beyond current state?)
- [ ] Is it achievable? (Dengan effort, could we realistically do this?)
- [ ] Is it aligned? (Does it support our mission/vision?)
- [ ] Is it time-bound? (Clear apa time period-nya?)

Score: If 5-6 checks, proceed. If 3-4, revise. If <3, start over.

**KEY RESULT Checklist:**

For each Key Result, verify:
- [ ] Is it measurable? (Ada clear number atau metric?)
- [ ] Is measurement method defined? (We know HOW to measure?)
- [ ] Is it outcome-focused? (Mengukur result, bukan activity?)
- [ ] Is it verifiable? (Objective, tidak subjective?)
- [ ] Is it relevant? (Contribute to the Objective?)
- [ ] Is baseline known? (Tahu starting point?)
- [ ] Is target realistic yet stretching? (70-80% confidence?)

Score per KR: If 6-7 checks, good. If 4-5, needs work. If <4, rewrite.

### 3.4 Creating Your OKR: Step-by-Step Workshop

Sekarang mari kita praktik membuat OKR untuk organisasi Anda. Ikuti step-by-step workshop ini.

#### **PREPARATION (Sebelum Workshop)**

**Materials needed:**
- Whiteboard atau large paper
- Sticky notes (different colors)
- Markers
- Laptop untuk dokumentasi
- Snacks! (Thinking needs energy)

**Who should participate:**
- Core leadership team (Ketua, Wakil, Sekretaris, Bendahara)
- Division heads
- Beberapa active members (untuk diverse perspective)
- Pembina/Advisor (optional, untuk guidance)

**Time allocation:**
Total 3-4 hours (bisa split jadi 2 sessions)

---

#### **STEP 1: Review Vision, Mission & Current State (30 menit)**

**Purpose:** Establish context dan shared understanding.

**Activities:**

**A. Vision/Mission Refresher**

Facilitator (Ketua) membuka dengan:
```
"Sebelum kita set OKRs, let's align pada why kita ada.

Our Vision: [State vision]
Our Mission: [State mission]

OKR yang kita buat today harus support vision dan mission ini."
```

**B. Current State Assessment**

Discuss:
- **Strengths:** Apa yang kita good at right now?
- **Weaknesses:** Apa yang masih struggle?
- **Opportunities:** Apa yang bisa kita manfaatkan?
- **Threats:** Apa yang potentially bisa challenge kita?

**Method: SWOT on sticky notes**
- 5 minutes: Individual brainstorming (tulis di sticky notes)
- 10 minutes: Tempel semua sticky notes di whiteboard, group by themes
- 10 minutes: Discuss patterns
- 5 minutes: Identify top 3 priorities dari discussion

**Example output:**
```
Top 3 Priorities:
1. Need to improve member retention (banyak yang join tapi keluar)
2. Program quality inconsistent (some events great, some meh)
3. Opportunity to expand external partnerships
```

---

#### **STEP 2: Envision Success - "6 Months From Now" (20 menit)**

**Purpose:** Dream tentang ideal future state.

**Activity: Guided Visualization**

Facilitator:
```
"Tutup mata. Bayangkan 6 bulan dari sekarang, semester ini telah berakhir dengan sangat sukses. 

- Apa yang telah kita capai?
- Bagaimana anggota merasakan organisasi?
- Apa yang orang lain (di luar organisasi) katakan tentang kita?
- Apa yang membuat kita bangga?

Buka mata. Tulis down di sticky notes."
```

**10 minutes:** Individual reflection dan writing

**10 minutes:** Share dan discuss

Look for patterns:
- Common themes yang muncul
- Aspirations yang resonate dengan banyak orang
- Concrete achievements yang disebutkan

#### Step 3: Brainstorm Objectives

Kumpulkan pengurus inti untuk brainstorming session.

**Question prompts:**
- "Apa yang mau kita achieve semester ini yang will make significant impact?"
- "6 bulan dari sekarang, apa yang mau kita proud of?"
- "Apa area yang paling critical untuk organisasi kita sekarang?"

**Tips brainstorming:**
- Jangan self-censor dulu. List semua ideas
- Think big, ambitious
- Focus pada outcome/impact, bukan activities

**Example brainstorming results untuk Himpunan Psikologi:**

Potential objectives:
1. Meningkatkan kualitas program akademik
2. Memperkuat networking dengan alumni dan profesional
3. Meningkatkan engagement dan retention anggota
4. Membangun brand awareness sebagai organisasi paling aktif
5. Meningkatkan kesejahteraan mental anggota
6. Mengembangkan kompetensi leadership pengurus

#### Step 4: Prioritize & Refine

Dari list objectives, pilih **3-5 yang paling critical**.

**Why tidak lebih banyak?**
- Focus is power. Too many objectives = diluted effort
- Organisasi mahasiswa punya limited resources (waktu, tenaga, budget)
- Better to achieve 3 objectives excellently than 10 objectives poorly

**Prioritization criteria:**
1. **Impact**: Seberapa besar difference-nya untuk organisasi?
2. **Urgency**: Seberapa critical timing-nya?
3. **Feasibility**: Dengan resources yang ada, realistic atau tidak?
4. **Alignment**: Seberapa aligned dengan vision/mission?

**Example prioritization untuk Himpunan Psikologi:**

**Top 3 Objectives untuk Semester Ini:**

**Objective 1:** Menjadikan organisasi sebagai wadah terbaik untuk pengembangan kompetensi praktis mahasiswa Psikologi

*Why priority?*
- High impact untuk career readiness anggota
- Urgent karena industry demand skills beyond teori
- Feasible dengan collaboration dengan profesional dan alumni
- Directly aligned dengan mission #1

**Objective 2:** Membangun komunitas yang solid dengan tingkat engagement dan kepuasan anggota tinggi

*Why priority?*
- Foundation untuk sustainability organisasi
- Retention issue adalah masalah ongoing
- Feasible dengan program internal
- Aligned dengan mission #3

**Objective 3:** Memperluas jangkauan dan impact organisasi di kampus dan beyond

*Why priority?*
- Increase relevance dan visibility
- Opens partnership opportunities
- Feasible dengan leveraging digital platforms
- Supports long-term growth

#### Step 5: Define Key Results untuk Setiap Objective

Untuk setiap objective, tentukan 3-5 Key Results yang measurable.

**Framework untuk brainstorm KR:**

Tanya untuk setiap objective:
1. "Bagaimana kita tahu kalau kita achieved objective ini?"
2. "Apa metrics yang could indicate success?"
3. "Apa leading indicators (actions) dan lagging indicators (results) yang relevant?"

**Example lengkap:**

---

**OBJECTIVE 1:** Menjadikan organisasi sebagai wadah terbaik untuk pengembangan kompetensi praktis mahasiswa Psikologi

**Key Results:**

**KR 1.1** (Leading indicator - Action based):
80% anggota aktif mengikuti minimal 2 workshop/pelatihan skill praktis (konseling, assessment, research methods) per semester

*How to measure:* Attendance tracking system

**KR 1.2** (Leading indicator - Action based):
Menghadirkan minimal 5 praktisi/profesional Psikologi untuk sharing session atau mentoring

*How to measure:* Event records

**KR 1.3** (Lagging indicator - Outcome based):
Tingkat kepuasan anggota terhadap program development mencapai 4.3/5.0 berdasarkan survey

*How to measure:* End-of-semester satisfaction survey

**KR 1.4** (Lagging indicator - Outcome based):
Minimal 30% anggota melaporkan bahwa mereka apply skills dari workshop dalam praktik (skripsi, magang, volunteer)

*How to measure:* Follow-up survey 1 bulan setelah workshop

---

**OBJECTIVE 2:** Membangun komunitas yang solid dengan tingkat engagement dan kepuasan anggota tinggi

**Key Results:**

**KR 2.1** (Leading indicator):
90% anggota hadir minimal di 3 dari 5 gathering/social events yang diadakan

*How to measure:* Attendance records

**KR 2.2** (Leading indicator):
Menjalankan program mentorship di mana 80% anggota baru mendapat mentor dari senior

*How to measure:* Mentorship program records

**KR 2.3** (Lagging indicator):
Retention rate anggota mencapai 85% (dari yang join di awal semester, berapa yang masih aktif di akhir semester)

*How to measure:* Compare active members list awal vs akhir semester

**KR 2.4** (Lagging indicator):
Net Promoter Score (NPS) organisasi minimal 50
(Survey: "Seberapa likely kamu recommend organisasi ini ke teman? 0-10")

*How to measure:* NPS survey

---

**OBJECTIVE 3:** Memperluas jangkauan dan impact organisasi di kampus dan beyond

**Key Results:**

**KR 3.1** (Leading indicator):
Mengadakan minimal 2 event open untuk umum (tidak hanya anggota) yang menarik minimal 100 participants per event

*How to measure:* Event registration and attendance data

**KR 3.2** (Leading indicator):
Publish minimal 2 artikel/content per minggu di Instagram yang provide value (tips, insights, educational content)

*How to measure:* Content calendar tracking

**KR 3.3** (Lagging indicator):
Instagram followers bertambah 50% (dari 2,000 menjadi 3,000) dengan engagement rate minimal 5%

*How to measure:* Instagram analytics

**KR 3.4** (Lagging indicator):
Mendapatkan minimal 1 partnership atau collaboration dengan organisasi eksternal (NGO, company, atau universitas lain)

*How to measure:* Partnership agreement documents

---

#### Step 6: Assign Ownership

Setiap KR harus ada yang "own" - bertanggung jawab untuk drive progress.

**Ownership structure:**

| Objective | Key Result | Owner (Primary) | Support Team |
|---|---|---|---|
| **O1:** Wadah pengembangan kompetensi | KR 1.1: 80% ikut 2 workshop | Divisi Pengembangan SDM | Divisi Internal |
| | KR 1.2: 5 praktisi sharing | Divisi Humas & Eksternal | Divisi Program |
| | KR 1.3: Kepuasan 4.3/5.0 | Ketua Umum | All divisions |
| | KR 1.4: 30% apply skills | Divisi Riset & Evaluasi | - |
| **O2:** Komunitas solid | KR 2.1: 90% hadir 3 events | Divisi Internal | - |
| | KR 2.2: 80% punya mentor | Divisi Kaderisasi | Divisi Internal |
| | KR 2.3: Retention 85% | Ketua Umum | All divisions |
| | KR 2.4: NPS minimal 50 | Divisi Riset & Evaluasi | - |
| **O3:** Jangkauan luas | KR 3.1: 2 event 100 pax | Divisi Program | Divisi Humas |
| | KR 3.2: 2 post/week | Divisi Komunikasi | - |
| | KR 3.3: Followers +50% | Divisi Komunikasi | Divisi Kreatif |
| | KR 3.4: 1 partnership | Divisi Humas & Eksternal | Ketua Umum |

**Responsibilities dari Owner:**
- Track progress regularly
- Communicate updates di check-in meetings
- Flag issues atau bottlenecks early
- Drive actions untuk achieve KR

### 3.5 Menjalankan OKR: Weekly & Monthly Rhythm

OKR bukan "set and forget". Butuh regular cadence untuk tracking dan adjustment.

#### Weekly Check-ins (15-30 menit)

**Format simple:**

**Opening (5 menit):**
- Quick mood check: "How is everyone doing?"
- Review last week's commitments

**Progress Update (15 menit):**
- Each KR owner share brief update:
  - Current status (in percentage atau metric)
  - What's working well
  - What's blocking atau challenging
  
**Example:**
```
"KR 1.1 - Workshop attendance:
- Current: 45% anggota sudah ikut 1 workshop, target 80% ikut 2 workshops
- What's working: Workshop content engaging, feedback positif
- Challenge: Scheduling conflicts dengan ujian, beberapa anggota belum aware
- Next actions: Reschedule 1 workshop, send personalized reminder"
```

**Commitments (5-10 menit):**
- What will each person do this week untuk move KRs forward?
- Write it down, review next week

**Tips:**
- Keep it short dan focused
- No blame, pure problem-solving
- If issue membutuhkan deeper discussion, schedule separate meeting

#### Monthly Reviews (1-2 jam)

**Format comprehensive:**

**Part 1: OKR Progress Review (45 menit)**

For each Objective:
1. Review all Key Results
   - Current score/percentage
   - On track, at risk, atau off track?
2. Celebrate wins (even small progress)
3. Analyze what's not working
   - Root cause?
   - Need resource reallocation?
   - Need strategy adjustment?

**Scoring system:**

Use color coding:
- üü¢ **Green (On track)**: 70%+ of expected progress untuk timeframe ini
- üü° **Yellow (At risk)**: 50-69% of expected progress, needs attention
- üî¥ **Red (Off track)**: <50% of expected progress, requires intervention

**Example monthly review:**

*Month 2 of 6-month OKR:*

**Objective 1: Wadah pengembangan kompetensi**

| Key Result | Target | Current | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| KR 1.1: 80% ikut 2 workshops | End of semester | 30% ikut 1 workshop | üü° Yellow | Need to accelerate |
| KR 1.2: 5 praktisi sharing | End of semester | 1 session done | üü¢ Green | On track |
| KR 1.3: Kepuasan 4.3/5.0 | End of semester | N/A (survey akhir) | üü¢ Green | Interim feedback positive |
| KR 1.4: 30% apply skills | End of semester | Too early to measure | - | - |

**Part 2: Action Planning (30 menit)**

For KRs yang yellow atau red:
- What specific actions will we take next month?
- Who owns each action?
- Any resources needed?
- Any obstacles to remove?

**Example action plan:**

*KR 1.1 At Risk (Yellow):*
```
Problem: Only 30% attended 1 workshop, need to accelerate to reach 80% attending 2

Root cause analysis:
- Many not aware of workshop value
- Scheduling conflicts
- Perception that workshops are "optional"

Action plan:
1. Create compelling video testimonials dari past participants (Owner: Divisi Kominfo, Deadline: Week 1)
2. Survey anggota untuk best scheduling (Owner: Divisi Riset, Deadline: Week 1)
3. Communicate that workshop attendance is KPI untuk active membership (Owner: Ketua Umum, Deadline: Week 2)
4. Offer makeup sessions atau recordings untuk yang tidak bisa hadir live (Owner: Divisi SDM, Deadline: Ongoing)
```

**Part 3: Reflection & Learning (15 menit)**

- What did we learn this month?
- What's working that we should do more?
- What's not working that we should stop or change?
- Any new insights atau opportunities?

#### Mid-Semester Check-in (2-3 jam)

At halfway point (Month 3), do deeper review:

**Questions to address:**

1. **Are our OKRs still relevant?**
   - Circumstances berubah?
   - Priorities shifted?
   - Should we adjust OKR?

2. **Are we on track untuk achieve 70-80%?**
   - If tracking untuk 100%: Maybe targets terlalu easy, consider stretching
   - If tracking untuk <50%: Maybe terlalu ambitious atau ada execution issues

3. **What's the mood dan energy level?**
   - Burnout indicators?
   - Need to adjust pace?

4. **Resource allocation appropriate?**
   - Some KRs over-resourced, some under-resourced?
   - Need rebalancing?

**Decision making:**

After mid-semester review, you might decide to:
- **Keep as is**: Everything on track
- **Adjust targets**: Increase atau decrease based on learning
- **Pivot strategy**: Change approach untuk achieve KR
- **Sunset KR**: Rare, but if KR truly not relevant anymore, oke untuk remove

**Important:** OKR is meant to be agile. Adjustment is not failure, it's learning.

### 3.6 Scoring & Celebrating OKR

End of semester: Time untuk final scoring dan reflection.

#### How to Score

**For each Key Result:**

Calculate achievement percentage:
```
Score = (Actual / Target) √ó 100%
```

**Example:**

KR 1.1: 80% anggota ikut 2 workshops
- Actual: 65% anggota ikut 2 workshops
- Score: (65/80) √ó 100% = 81.25%

**For each Objective:**

Average score dari all Key Results under that Objective:
```
Objective Score = Average of all KR scores
```

**Example:**

Objective 1: Wadah pengembangan kompetensi
- KR 1.1 score: 81.25%
- KR 1.2 score: 100% (got 6 praktisi instead of 5)
- KR 1.3 score: 95.56% (got 4.3 dari target 4.5)
- KR 1.4 score: 75% (22.5% apply dari target 30%)

Objective 1 Score = (81.25 + 100 + 95.56 + 75) / 4 = **87.95%**

#### Interpreting Scores

| Score Range | Interpretation | Next Steps |
|---|---|---|
| **90-100%** | Exceeded expectations! | Consider setting more ambitious targets next time |
| **70-89%** | Great! Hit the stretch goal zone | Maintain momentum, celebrate success |
| **50-69%** | Decent progress, but could be better | Analyze what held back, improve execution |
| **30-49%** | Significant underachievement | Deep dive into what went wrong, major changes needed |
| **0-29%** | Did not achieve | Fundamental issue - wrong goal, wrong strategy, or execution breakdown |

**Important context:**

Scores harus dilihat dengan context:
- Did external circumstances change dramatically? (e.g., pandemi, kebijakan kampus)
- Were there resource constraints? (budget cuts, key members leaving)
- Were assumptions wrong? (target population, market response)

#### Celebration Ritual

**Why celebrate?**
- Recognize effort and achievement
- Build positive momentum
- Reinforce culture of execution excellence
- Boost morale untuk next cycle

**How to celebrate:**

**1. Public Recognition**
```
Organize end-of-semester gathering:
- Present OKR results dengan pride
- Highlight top performers dan divisions
- Share success stories dan testimonials
- Give certificates atau awards
```

**2. Document Success**
```
Create "Achievements Report":

- Key highlights dan memorable moments
- Lessons learned
- Share di social media, internal channels, dan ke stakeholders
```

**3. Team Celebration**
```
- Dinner atau outing together
- Fun awards (bukan hanya formal):
  * "Most Consistent Performer"
  * "Best Comeback" (worst start, best finish)
  * "Hidden MVP" (yang kerjanya tidak flashy tapi critical)
  * "Innovation Award" (most creative solution)
```

**4. Individual Appreciation**
```

"[Name], your dedication untuk drive KR X truly made difference. The way you
```

### 3.7 Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

#### Pitfall 1: Too Many OKRs

**Problem:**
```
Organization set 8 Objectives dengan total 35 Key Results.
Result: Overwhelmed, diluted focus, achieved nothing excellently
```

**Solution:**
- **Limit to 3-5 Objectives max**
- Each Objective max 5 Key Results
- Remember: Less is more. Better excellent execution on few than mediocre on many

#### Pitfall 2: Confusing Activities with Key Results

**Problem:**
```
‚ùå Bad KR: "Conduct 4 meetings per month"
(This is activity, not result)

‚ùå Bad KR: "Create social media content plan"
(This is task, not measurable outcome)
```

**Solution:**
```
‚úÖ Good KR: "Increase meeting effectiveness: 80% of action items from meetings completed within deadline"
(Focus on outcome of meetings)

‚úÖ Good KR: "Social media engagement rate reaches 8% with 500+ interactions per week"
(Focus on outcome of content, not creation of plan)
```

**Rule of thumb:** KR should answer "so what?"
- "We conducted 4 meetings" ‚Üí So what? What changed?
- "We created content plan" ‚Üí So what? What impact?

#### Pitfall 3: Set and Forget

**Problem:**
```
Organization set beautiful OKRs di awal semester.
No review until end of semester.
Result: Totally off track, no adjustment made, learning opportunity lost
```

**Solution:**
- **Weekly check-ins**: Non-negotiable, even if brief
- **Monthly reviews**: Deep dive
- **Mid-semester assessment**: Adjustment point
- Make OKR visible: Dashboard yang semua bisa lihat anytime

**Pro tip:** Create physical atau digital OKR board di ruang organisasi
- Visual reminder
- Transparency
- Creates accountability

#### Pitfall 4: Not Aligning OKRs Across Levels

**Problem:**
```
Organizational OKR: "Become most impactful org on campus"
Division A OKR: "Increase our Instagram followers" (not directly support org OKR)
Division B OKR: "Have fun team building events" (nice but not critical to org OKR)
```

**Solution:**

**Cascading OKRs:**

```
Level 1: Organizational OKR
‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ Level 2: Divisional OKRs (support Level 1)
    ‚îî‚îÄ‚îÄ Level 3: Individual OKRs (support Level 2)
```

**Example:**

**Organizational OKR:**
O: Become most impactful student org in developing future-ready skills
- KR: 70% members report significant skill improvement
- KR: Partner with 3 companies for practical training
- KR: 500+ external students attend our workshops

**Division OKR (aligned):**

*Divisi Program:*
O: Design dan execute high-quality skill development programs
- KR: Run 8 workshops with satisfaction 4.5/5
- KR: Secure 3 industry expert speakers
- KR: 85% workshop attendance rate

*Divisi Humas:*
O: Build partnerships that provide access to industry
- KR: Reach out to 30 companies, convert 3 partnerships
- KR: Maintain relationship dengan 5 alumni di industry
- KR: Generate 2 collaboration opportunities per semester

*Divisi Kominfo:*
O: Amplify reach untuk attract external participants
- KR: 500+ registrations untuk open events
- KR: Instagram followers +60% dengan 6% engagement
- KR: 2 viral posts (10k+ reach each)

See how semua divisional OKRs ladder up ke organizational OKR?

#### Pitfall 5: Punishing Failure

**Problem:**
```
Organization culture: "Kalau tidak achieve OKR, ada consequences"
Result: 
- People set easy targets (sandbag)
- People gaming the numbers
- Fear of taking risks
- No honest reporting
```

**Solution:**

**OKR is learning tool, bukan punishment tool.**

Messaging from leadership:
```
"OKRs are stretch goals. Achieving 70-80% is success. The point is untuk push 
ourselves beyond comfort zone dan learn.

If you achieved 100%, that means target was too easy. Next time be more ambitious.

If you achieved 40%, let's understand why:
- Was goal unrealistic?
- Were there unforeseen obstacles?
- Did strategy not work?
- What did we learn?

There's no punishment untuk not achieving OKR. The only 'punishment' adalah kalau 
we don't learn dari the experience."
```

**Celebrate learning dari failure:**
- "Best Learning Award" untuk team yang extracted most valuable lessons dari missed OKR
- Post-mortem yang fokus pada insight, bukan blame
- Encourage experimentation: "We tried X, didn't work, learned Y"

- KR 1: 4 program sosial (santunan, teaching, environment) dengan 200+ beneficiaries direct impact

‚úÖ **Objective yang baik karena:**

- **Qualitative**: Fokus pada outcome (soft skills development), bukan output (jumlah workshop)
- **Meaningful**: Connect dengan purpose yang bigger
- **Time-bound**: Jelas untuk semester ini

‚úÖ **Key Results yang baik karena:**

**KR 1 (80% anggota ikut 2 workshop):**
- **Measurable**: Ada angka jelas (80%, 2 workshop)
- **Achievable tapi challenging**: 80% adalah stretch, bukan easy 50%
- **Relevant**: Workshop adalah cara konkret develop soft skills
- **Leading indicator**: Action yang drive outcome

**KR 2 (Kepuasan 4.5/5.0):**
- **Measurable**: Quantifiable satisfaction score
- **Quality-focused**: Bukan hanya quantity, tapi quality matters
- **Lagging indicator**: Outcome dari program yang baik

**KR 3 (90% alumni report impact):**
- **Long-term oriented**: Tidak hanya saat masih aktif, tapi impact lasting

### 3.10 OKR untuk Leader: Personal Development

Jangan lupa, sebagai leader, kamu juga perlu OKR untuk personal development.

**Example Personal Leadership OKR:**

**Objective:** Menjadi leader yang transformatif dan trusted oleh tim

**Key Results:**
- KR 1: Conduct one-on-one check-in dengan semua 12 anggota tim inti minimal 1x per bulan (target: 100% completion rate)
- KR 2: 360-degree feedback score dari tim minimal 4.5/5.0 dalam aspek communication, support, dan fairness
- KR 3: Respond to all messages atau concerns dari anggota dalam maksimal 24 jam (target: 95% on-time response rate)
- KR 4: Facilitate minimal 2 learning sessions untuk team development
- KR 5: Personal learning: Complete 2 leadership books/courses dan apply minimal 3 new practices

Track ini privately atau share dengan mentor/pembina untuk accountability.

---

**Kesimpulan Bab 3:**

OKR adalah powerful tool untuk transform "busy organization" menjadi "impactful organization". Kunci suksesnya bukan pada perfect OKR formulation, tapi pada:

1. **Consistent execution** - Weekly rhythm, monthly reviews
2. **Transparency** - Everyone tahu progress
3. **Learning mindset** - Failure is data, bukan disaster
4. **Flexibility** - Adjust kalau perlu based on learning
5. **Celebration** - Recognize effort dan achievement

Start simple. Pilih 2-3 objectives. Track dengan disiplin. Learn and improve. Seiring waktu, OKR akan jadi second nature untuk organisasi kalian.
