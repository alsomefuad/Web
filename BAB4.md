## BAB 4: MENGELOLA KONFLIK KONSTRUKTIF

### 4.1 Pengantar: Mengapa Konflik Itu Normal (dan Bisa Jadi Baik)

**Mindset shift yang perlu terjadi:**

❌ **Mindset lama:**
"Organisasi yang baik = tidak ada konflik"
"Konflik = gagal sebagai leader"
"Kalau semua harmonis, berarti organisasi sehat"

✅ **Mindset baru:**
"Konflik adalah natural ketika ada orang yang peduli dan punya pendapat berbeda"
"Tidak ada konflik might mean tidak ada care atau tidak ada honesty"
"Yang berbahaya bukan konfliknya, tapi konflik yang tidak dimanage"

**Why konflik could be good:**

1. **Sign of engagement**: Orang yang tidak care tidak akan argue. Konflik seringkali berarti people are invested.

2. **Innovation catalyst**: Friction of ideas menghasilkan better solutions. Groupthink adalah musuh innovation.

3. **Relationship deepener**: Kalau konflik di-resolve dengan sehat, relationship jadi lebih kuat dan trust meningkat.

4. **Learning opportunity**: Konflik expose blind spots, assumption, dan areas untuk improvement.

**Analogy:**
Konflik seperti warning light di dashboard mobil. Tidak enak dilihat, tapi memberitahu ada yang perlu attention. Kalau diignore, mobil bisa rusak. Kalau di-address, mobil jadi lebih sehat.

### 4.2 Tiga Jenis Konflik di Organisasi

Tidak semua konflik sama. Recognizing tipe konflik helps dalam determining strategy resolution.

#### Tipe 1: Task Conflict (Konflik Tugas)

**Definisi:** Perbedaan pendapat tentang content atau approach pekerjaan

**Karakteristik:**
- Focus pada WHAT atau HOW, bukan WHO
- Bisa di-resolve dengan data, logic, atau testing
- Sebenarnya HEALTHY kalau dimanage dengan baik

**Example situations:**

**Situation 1: Marketing Strategy**
```
Person A: "Kita harus fokus promosi di Instagram Stories"
Person B: "Menurutku Instagram Feed post lebih effective untuk reach"
```

**Situation 2: Event Format**
```
Person A: "Seminar harusnya online biar lebih banyak yang bisa join"
Person B: "Offline lebih engaging dan ada networking opportunity"
```

**Why this is healthy:**
Kedua pihak argue untuk kepentingan organisasi, bukan personal agenda. Ini adalah intellectual discourse.

**How to handle:**

✅ **Approach 1: Data-driven decision**
```
"Both ideas punya merit. Let's look at data:
- Instagram Stories: Average views 150, swipe-up rate 3%
- Feed posts: Average reach 500, engagement rate 5%

Based on past performance, Feed posts seems more effective. But let's try A/B testing:
Week 1-2: Focus Stories
Week 3-4: Focus Feed
Compare results, then decide."
```

✅ **Approach 2: Combine ideas**
```
"What if we do both? Stories untuk real-time updates dan Behind The Scenes. 
Feed untuk main announcements. They serve different purposes."
```

✅ **Approach 3: Experiment**
```
"We're not sure which works better. Let's run pilot:
- Event A: online
- Event B: offline
Track attendance, engagement, satisfaction. Learn dari both."
```

**Red flags that task conflict is turning unhealthy:**
- Personal attacks: "Ide kamu selalu yang aneh-aneh"
- Stubborn without reason: "Pokoknya harus cara saya"
- Power play: "Saya senior, jadi dengarkan saya"

#### Tipe 2: Process Conflict (Konflik Proses)

**Definisi:** Kebingungan atau disagreement tentang WHO does WHAT, WHEN, dan HOW (workflow)

**Karakteristik:**
- Root cause: Unclear roles atau poor coordination
- Symptom: Duplicated work, dropped balls, finger-pointing
- Usually not personal, tapi bisa escalate jadi personal kalau not addressed

**Example situations:**

**Situation 1: Overlapping Responsibilities**
```
Issue: Proposal untuk sponsor harus dibuat.

Divisi Humas: "Itu tugas Divisi Program, mereka yang tahu detail acara"
Divisi Program: "Itu tugas Humas, mereka yang biasa deal sama sponsor"

Result: Deadline lewat, tidak ada yang buat proposal
```

**Situation 2: Approval Bottleneck**
```
Issue: Design poster sudah jadi, perlu approval sebelum publish.

Designer: "Saya tunggu approval dari Ketua"
Ketua: "Saya kira Kepala Divisi yang approve"
Kepala Divisi: "Saya tunggu Ketua lihat dulu"

Result: Poster terlambat published, momen promotional terlewat
```

**How to handle:**

✅ **Solution 1: Clarify RACI Matrix**

RACI = Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed

| Task | Responsible (Do the work) | Accountable (Final decision) | Consulted (Input) | Informed (FYI) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Buat proposal sponsor | Divisi Humas | Ketua Umum | Divisi Program, Bendahara | All divisions |
| Design promotional material | Divisi Kreatif | Kepala Div. Kominfo | Ketua Umum | - |
| Approve budget expenditure >1jt | Bendahara | Ketua Umum | - | Sekretaris |

**How to create RACI:**
```
Step 1: List all recurring tasks atau common activities
Step 2: For each task, identify:
        - Who actually does the work? (R)
        - Who has final say/accountability? (A) - should be only 1 person
        - Who needs to be consulted for input? (C)
        - Who just needs to know outcome? (I)
Step 3: Document dalam SOP
Step 4: Onboard semua anggota tentang RACI
Step 5: Review dan update every semester
```

✅ **Solution 2: Clear Workflow & Timeline**

For repetitive processes, create standard workflow.

**Example: Event Approval Workflow**

```
STEP 1: Initiator (Divisi Program)
- Submit event proposal ke Ketua Umum
- Deadline: H-30 dari event
- Format: Use template X
- Include: Concept, budget, timeline

STEP 2: Review (Ketua Umum + Bendahara)
- Review proposal dalam 3 hari kerja
- Provide feedback atau approval
- If revision needed: back to Step 1
- If approved: proceed to Step 3

STEP 3: Planning (Divisi terkait)
- Breakdown tasks dengan deadline
- Update di Trello board
- Weekly progress update ke Ketua

STEP 4: Pre-event Check (H-7)
- Final walkthrough dengan semua divisi
- Contingency plan review
- Go/no-go decision

STEP 5: Post-event (H+3)
- Evaluation meeting
- Financial report
- Lessons learned documentation
```

With clear workflow, everyone tahu:
- What happens when
- Who's responsible at each stage
- What's the expected timeline
- Where to escalate if stuck

✅ **Solution 3: Regular Coordination Meetings**

Process conflicts often arise dari lack of communication.

**Coordination meeting structure:**

**Weekly Sync (30 menit):**
- Each division share: What we're working on, Any dependencies dari divisi lain, Any blockers
- Identify overlaps atau gaps
- Coordinate handoffs

**Example:**
```
Divisi Program: "Kami finalize rundown acara hari Rabu. Divisi Humas, we need confirmed 
                 speakers list untuk di-announce. Bisa ready Selasa?"
Divisi Humas:   "Oke, kami prioritaskan. Tapi kami butuh approved budget dari Bendahara 
                 untuk booking fee."
Bendahara:      "Budget approval bisa hari ini juga. Will process after this meeting."
```

This prevents "I didn't know you needed that" situations.

#### Tipe 3: Relationship Conflict (Konflik Hubungan)

**Definisi:** Personal friction atau incompatibility antar individu

**Karakteristik:**
- Tentang WHO, bukan WHAT atau HOW
- Emotional, not rational
- Most dangerous type kalau not addressed
- Bisa contaminate seluruh organisasi

**Example situations:**

**Situation 1: Personality Clash**
```
Person A: Ekstrovert, spontan, casual
Person B: Introvert, terencana, formal

A's perception: "B terlalu kaku dan boring. Tidak fun bekerja dengannya."
B's perception: "A terlalu chaotic dan tidak profesional. Bikin stress."

Manifestation:
- Avoid each other
- Passive-aggressive comments
- Refuse to collaborate
- Talk behind back
```

**Situation 2: Historical Baggage**
```
Backstory: 6 bulan lalu, Si C kritik kerja Si D di depan umum. D merasa embarrassed 
           dan angry, tapi tidak pernah address.

Current: C dan D di-assign untuk same project.

Result:
- D cold shoulder C, minimal communication
- C frustrated karena D tidak kooperatif
- Project terganggu
```

**Situation 3: Perceived Unfairness**
```
Perception: Si E merasa Si F selalu dapat tugas easy dan appreciation banyak, 
            sementara E kerja keras tapi tidak dilihat.

Feeling: Resentment, jealousy

Manifestation:
- E make snide comments tentang F
- E less motivated
- E gossip to others tentang favoritism
```

**Why relationship conflict is dangerous:**

1. **Emotional contagion**: Negative emotions spread. Other members pick sides, team splits.

2. **Performance impact**: Hard to collaborate effectively kalau ada personal animosity.

3. **Retention risk**: Talented people leave because "environment toxic".

4. **Reputation damage**: Drama leaks out, organizational reputation suffer.

**How to handle:**

This is most challenging. Requires intervention, usually dari leader atau neutral third party.

**Step-by-Step Conflict Resolution Process:**

**STEP 1: Assess Severity & Urgency**

| Severity | Indicators | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Low** | Minor annoyance, tidak affect work significantly | Monitor, offer coaching |
| **Medium** | Tension evident, starting to affect collaboration | Mediation needed soon |
| **High** | Open hostility, work severely impacted, others affected | Immediate intervention |

**STEP 2: Prepare for Conversation**

Before meeting dengan conflicted parties:

**As mediator, prepare:**
- [ ] Understand both sides: Talk to each person individually first
- [ ] Gather facts: What actually happened vs perceptions
- [ ] Check yourself: Am I biased toward one party?
- [ ] Set intention: Goal is resolution, not judgment
- [ ] Plan logistics: Private space, adequate time (1-2 hours), neutral ground

**Tips:**
- Don't ambush. Give heads up: "I notice ada tension. Can we talk tentang it?"
- Frame positively: "I want to help resolve this so we can all work better together"
- Assure confidentiality: "What's shared here stays here, kecuali ada agreement untuk share"

**STEP 3: Facilitate Conversation Menggunakan STATE Framework**

STATE adalah conversation framework untuk discuss difficult issues.

**S - Share Your Facts**

Start dengan observable facts, bukan interpretasi atau accusation.

❌ Poor:
"Kamu selalu nyinyir dan tidak pernah support saya"
(Subjective, accusatory, "always" is exaggeration)

✅ Better:
"Dalam 3 rapat terakhir, saya notice ketika saya share ide, kamu langsung point out 
weaknesses tanpa acknowledge potential-nya. Specifically, [give 2-3 concrete examples]"
(Factual, specific, observable)

**Mediator's role:**
```
"Let's start dengan facts. [Person A], can you describe specific situations atau 
behaviors yang bothering you? Use 'I observed' atau 'I noticed' language."
```

**T - Tell Your Story**

Share your interpretation dan how it affected you emotionally.

❌ Poor:
"Kamu memang orangnya suka merendahkan orang lain"
(Character assassination, mind reading)

✅ Better:
"Ketika kamu langsung point out kelemahan tanpa acknowledge potential, saya merasa 
ide saya tidak dihargai dan saya jadi ragu untuk share ide di masa depan. Saya merasa 
seperti kontribusi saya tidak valued."
(Share interpretation dan emotional impact)

**Mediator's role:**
```
"Thank you for sharing your facts. Now, [Person A], can you share how those situations 
affected you emotionally? What story did you tell yourself tentang those behaviors?"

Listen for:
- Feelings (frustrated, hurt, angry, disappointed)
- Interpretations (what they think the behavior means)
- Impact on them (how it affected their work, confidence, relationships)
```

**A - Ask for Others' Paths**

Setelah share your perspective, invite the other person untuk share theirs.

❌ Poor:
"Itu fakta yang terjadi. Kamu tidak bisa deny it."
(Closed, adversarial, tidak ada space untuk dialogue)

✅ Better:
"That's my experience dan how I felt about it. Saya mau understand dari kamu: 
What was going on from your perspective? What did you intend?"
(Open, curious, create space untuk their story)

**Why this matters:**

Seringkali konflik terjadi karena **attribution error**:
- We judge others by their actions, but judge ourselves by our intentions
- Person A might think: "Dia sengaja nyinyir untuk merendahkan saya"
- Person B's actual intention: "Saya cuma mau ensure quality dengan point out risks"

Kalau tidak ask, misunderstanding terus berlanjut.

**Mediator's role:**
```
"[Person B], we've heard [Person A]'s perspective. I'm curious tentang your side. 
What was your intention dalam those moments? What were you trying to achieve?"

Guide them to explain:
- What they were thinking
- What they were trying to achieve
- Context that Person A might not know
- Their own feelings dalam situation tersebut
```

**Example dialogue:**

```
Person A: "Saya merasa kamu selalu shoot down ide saya"

Person B: "Oh, I didn't realize you felt that way. Dari perspektif saya, I was trying 
to help make the idea stronger. I thought dengan point out potential issues early, 
kita bisa anticipate dan solve them. Tapi saya see now how that came across as dismissive. 
That wasn't my intention at all. Saya actually think you have great creative ideas, 
that's why I engage so much."

Person A: "I didn't know you see it that way. Saya thought you just didn't like my ideas."
```

See how asking opens up understanding?

**T - Talk Tentatively**

Present your views as perspectives, not absolute truths.

❌ Poor:
"Kamu itu toxic dan egois. Itu fakta."
(Stated as certainty, no room untuk dialogue, character assassination)

✅ Better:
"Dari observasi saya, beberapa behavior seperti [specific example] could be perceived 
as [negative impact]. Saya might be wrong, tapi that's how I'm experienced it. 
Help me understand kalau interpretation saya off."
(Tentative, open to correction, focus on behavior bukan character)

**Language that shows tentativeness:**

- "Dari perspektif saya..."
- "Saya could be wrong, but..."
- "One possible interpretation is..."
- "Help me understand kalau saya missing something..."
- "Maybe there's context I don't know..."

**Why this matters:**

Tentative language:
- **Reduces defensiveness**: Easier untuk person dengar kalau not attacked
- **Invites correction**: Shows you're open untuk learn
- **Maintains relationship**: Not positioning yourself as judge
- **Encourages honesty**: Safe untuk them admit kalau ada yang salah

**Mediator's role:**

Model tentative language dan guide parties to use it:

```
❌ "You need to stop saying 'He always does X'. That's an accusation."

✅ "Let's rephrase that. Instead of 'He always does X', how about 'I've noticed 
X happening in these specific situations: [examples]'. That way we're talking 
about specific behaviors we can address, not character judgments."
```

**E - Encourage Testing**

End dengan concrete action plan untuk test resolution.

**Structure:**

1. **Summarize shared understanding:**
   "So what I'm hearing is: [Person A] felt [X] when [behavior] happened. [Person B] 
   intended [Y] dan didn't realize impact. Both of you want [shared goal]. Accurate?"

2. **Agree on changes:**
   "What specific behaviors would each of you like to see differently?"
   
   Example agreements:
   - Person B: "I'll make sure untuk acknowledge positive aspects ide dulu before 
     point out concerns. And I'll ask 'Can I share some thoughts?' rather than 
     jump in uninvited."
   - Person A: "I'll be more explicit about whether I want feedback atau just brainstorming. 
     And I'll assume positive intent dan ask for clarification kalau something feels off."

3. **Set check-in:**
   "Let's try these changes for 2 minggu. We'll meet again on [date] untuk discuss 
   how it's going. During this time, both of you commit to give each other benefit 
   of doubt dan communicate directly kalau ada issue."

4. **Define success:**
   "What would success look like? How will we know this is resolved?"
   
   Example success criteria:
   - Person A feels comfortable sharing ideas di meetings
   - Person B's feedback is received as constructive, bukan critical
   - They can disagree productively without personal friction
   - Team meetings become more collaborative

**Mediator's role - FOLLOW UP:**

Ini adalah CRUCIAL tapi sering di-skip.

```
Two weeks later:

Email to both parties:
"Hi [Names],

It's been 2 minggu since our conversation. I want to check in:

- How have things been going?
- Have you noticed any positive changes?
- Any challenges atau misunderstandings yang perlu di-address?
- Do we need another conversation, atau things are improving?

Please share honest feedback. This is progress check, bukan judgment.

Best,
[Mediator]"
```

**Possible outcomes:**

**Scenario 1: Significant improvement**
```
Acknowledge success dan encourage maintenance:
"Great progress! What you're both doing is working. Let's keep the momentum. 
Feel free to reach out kalau ada setback."
```

**Scenario 2: Some improvement, some struggle**
```
Another conversation to troubleshoot:
"Good to hear ada progress di [area X]. Let's talk about [area Y] yang masih 
challenging. What's getting in the way?"
```

**Scenario 3: No improvement atau worse**
```
Escalate atau make tough decision:
"Meski sudah ada conversation dan agreement, situation tidak improve. We need to 
consider other options: [restructuring team, separating them, atau dalam kasus 
ekstrim, asking one to leave]. This isn't working untuk anyone."
```

### **FULL STATE FRAMEWORK SUMMARY**

| Element | What it Means | Example Language |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| **Share Facts** | Describe observable behaviors without interpretation | "In the last 3 meetings, when I shared ideas, you immediately pointed out problems without acknowledging positives" |
| **Tell Story** | Share your interpretation dan emotional impact | "I interpreted that as you not valuing my contributions, and I felt discouraged from participating" |
| **Ask Others' Paths** | Invite their perspective dan understand their intention | "What was going on from your side? What were you trying to achieve?" |
| **Talk Tentatively** | Present views as perspectives, not absolute truths | "I could be wrong, but that's how I experienced it. Help me understand if I'm missing context." |
| **Encourage Testing** | Create action plan dan commit to follow-up | "Let's try these specific changes for 2 weeks and check in on [date] to see how it's working" |
