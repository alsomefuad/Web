## BAB 7: BUDAYA KESEJAHTERAAN (WELLNESS CULTURE)

### 7.1 Pengantar: Krisis Diam-Diam di Organisasi Mahasiswa

**Skenario yang terlalu sering terjadi:**

```
SEMESTER AWAL: Semangat Tinggi
"Kita bakal bikin organisasi ini amazing!"
- Meeting 3x seminggu
- Chat grup aktif 24/7
- Volunteer untuk semua tugas
- "Yes, I can do it!"

BULAN KE-3: Mulai Kelelahan
- Meeting attendance turun
- Response di chat melambat
- "Maaf ya, lagi banyak tugas kuliah"
- Mulai ada yang sering izin

BULAN KE-4-5: Burnout Massal
- Anggota sakit bergantian
- Motivasi menurun drastis
- Kualitas kerja drop
- "Aku cape banget, pengen resign"
- Yang bertahan hanya karena "tidak enak" resign

AKHIR SEMESTER: Organisasi Survive, Tapi...
- 60% anggota awal sudah keluar
- Yang stay mostly karena obligation, bukan passion
- "Organisasi ini toxic"
- Regenerasi susah karena reputation buruk
```

**Ini adalah tanda-tanda organizational wellness crisis.**

### Apa itu Budaya Kesejahteraan?

**Definisi:**
> Budaya Kesejahteraan adalah lingkungan organisasi yang secara intentional dan sistematis memprioritaskan physical health, mental well-being, dan work-life balance dari semua anggotanya.

Bukan hanya:
- ‚ùå "Kalau kamu stress, istirahat aja" (reactive)
- ‚ùå "Kesehatan mental itu penting" (lip service tanpa action)
- ‚ùå Satu kali team outing terus claim "kita care sama well-being"

Tapi:
- ‚úÖ **Proactive systems** yang prevent burnout sejak awal
- ‚úÖ **Consistent practices** yang embedded dalam operasional
- ‚úÖ **Leadership yang role model** healthy behaviors
- ‚úÖ **Safe environment** di mana vulnerability is welcomed
- ‚úÖ **Sustainable pace** yang realistis untuk mahasiswa

### Mengapa Ini Penting (Terutama untuk Gen Z)?

Kita sudah bahas di Bab 1 bahwa Gen Z rentan terhadap:
- Burnout
- Overthinking & anxiety
- Comparison & imposter syndrome
- FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)
- Digital exhaustion

**Data context:**

Berdasarkan berbagai studi tentang mahasiswa Indonesia:
- **70%+** mahasiswa melaporkan stress level tinggi
- **40%+** pernah experience anxiety atau depresi
- **60%+** merasa overwhelmed dengan commitments
- **Hanya 20%** yang actively seek help saat struggle

**Implikasi untuk organisasi:**

Kalau organisasi **tidak** intentionally create wellness culture:
1. **Retention problem**: Talent-talent terbaik pergi
2. **Performance decline**: Burnout = productivity drop
3. **Toxic culture develops**: Unhealthy competition, gossip, blame
4. **Reputation damage**: "Jangan join organisasi itu, toxic"
5. **Leadership crisis**: Tidak ada yang mau jadi pengurus karena takut overwhelmed

Kalau organisasi **does** create wellness culture:
1. **Higher retention**: Orang stay karena feel cared for
2. **Better performance**: Rested people = creative, productive people
3. **Positive culture**: Support, collaboration, psychological safety
4. **Good reputation**: "Organisasi itu supportive banget!"
5. **Leadership pipeline**: Orang excited untuk step up karena tahu organisasi support them

### The Reality Check

**Organizational wellness bukan luxury, ini adalah necessity untuk sustainability.**

Analogi: Organisasi seperti marathon, bukan sprint. Kalau runner lari full speed dari awal tanpa pacing atau hydration breaks, they will collapse sebelum finish line.

### 7.2 Tiga Dimensi Kesejahteraan Organisasi

#### Dimensi 1: **Physical Wellness** (Kesehatan Fisik)

**Kenapa ini matters dalam konteks organisasi mahasiswa?**

Mahasiswa yang aktif berorganisasi often:
- Kurang tidur (meeting malam, deadline midnight)
- Makan tidak teratur (skip meals karena sibuk)
- Kurang olahraga (waktu habis untuk kuliah + organisasi)
- Posture buruk (sitting di laptop berjam-jam)

**Long-term consequences:**
- Immune system lemah ‚Üí sering sakit
- Energy level rendah ‚Üí produktivitas drop
- Physical pain (back pain, eye strain, headaches)
- Mental health affected (physical & mental health are interconnected)

**What organizations can do:**

**A. Scheduling yang Sehat**

```
PRINSIP: Respect biological needs

‚ùå BAD PRACTICES:
- Meeting jam 10 malam keatas (sleep deprivation)
- Back-to-back meetings tanpa break
- Weekend penuh dengan activities (no rest)
- All-nighter culture ("Real pengurus itu begadang!")

‚úÖ GOOD PRACTICES:
- Meeting time: maksimal jam 9 malam (earlier better)
- Buffer time: minimal 15 menit between meetings
- Meeting duration: max 90 menit, dengan break 5-10 menit di tengah
- Weekend respect: minimal 1 hari full off per week
- Discourage all-nighters: "Besok aja dengan kepala fresh lebih baik"
```

**Implementation example:**

```
ORGANIZATIONAL POLICY: HEALTHY SCHEDULING

1. MEETING TIME BOUNDARIES
   - Working hours: 08.00 - 21.00
   - After 21.00 adalah personal time (kecuali emergency)
   - Weekend meeting hanya kalau absolutely necessary dan di-inform minimal H-3

2. MEETING STRUCTURE
   - Maximum duration: 90 menit
   - Setiap 45 menit: 5 menit stretch break
   - Agenda clear, sent H-1 minimal
   - Start on time, end on time (respect everyone's schedule)

3. DEADLINE MANAGEMENT
   - No deadline yang require orang work past midnight
   - Buffer time always included (jangan mepet-mepet)
   - Weekend deadline only for critical matters dengan approval

4. BUSY SEASON MANAGEMENT
   - During exam periods: organizational activities paused atau minimal
   - Major event: ensure adequate recovery time after (min 1 week low-intensity)

5. SIGNS OF OVERWORK TO WATCH
   - Anggota consistently hadir dengan kantong mata atau look exhausted
   - Performance quality drop
   - Frequent sick leave
   
   ‚Üí Leader action: Check-in conversation, adjust workload
```

**B. Meeting Wellness Practices**

```
PRE-MEETING:
- Share agenda beforehand
- Ensure meeting is actually necessary (could it be email/chat?)
- Check: apakah everyone sudah makan? Kalau meeting time = meal time, provide snacks

DURING MEETING:
- Start dengan quick mood check: "Scale 1-5, how's everyone doing today?"
- Snacks & drinks available
- Mid-meeting stretch break (literally stand up, stretch 2 minutes)
- Encourage bathroom breaks (don't make people wait till end)
- Posture reminder: "Let's check our sitting posture"

POST-MEETING:
- Clear action items (jangan biarkan orang pulang confused)
- Realistic timeline untuk follow-ups
- "Take care, see you next time!" (end on positive note)
```

**C. Encourage Physical Activity**

Organisasi bisa facilitate, bukan mandatkan.

```
IDEAS:

1. WEEKLY GROUP EXERCISE (Optional)
   - Morning jog bareng setiap Sabtu pagi
   - Yoga/stretching session (bisa virtual via Zoom)
   - Futsal atau basketball every 2 weeks
   
   Key: FUN, not competitive. Bonding + health.

2. ACTIVE MEETINGS (Occasional)
   - "Walking meeting" untuk 1-on-1 chats
   - Standing meetings (stay on point, tidak terlalu lama)

3. MOVEMENT CHALLENGES (Fun gamification)
   - "30-Day Step Challenge" dengan app tracking
   - Prizes untuk participation (not just winners)
   - Share progress di group chat untuk motivation

4. ERGONOMICS AWARENESS
   - Share tips tentang proper sitting posture
   - Encourage breaks every 45-60 minutes
   - Remind: eye health (20-20-20 rule: every 20 min, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds)
```

**D. Nutrition Support**

```
Small gestures dengan big impact:

1. MEETING SNACKS
   - Provide healthy snacks di meetings (buah, nuts, bukan hanya gorengan)
   - Hydration: always ada air minum

2. MEAL TIME RESPECT
   - Jangan schedule meeting pas lunch/dinner time
   - Kalau unavoidable: provide meals
   - Check: "Udah makan belum?" bukan cuma basa-basi, tapi genuine care

3. SHARED MEALS (Occasional)
   - Monthly team lunch/dinner together
   - Potluck style (affordable, fun)
   - Bonding + ensure everyone eat properly

4. FOOD ALLERGY/PREFERENCE AWARENESS
   - Tahu kalau ada yang vegetarian, allergies, atau dietary restrictions
   - Accommodate saat provide food
```

#### Dimensi 2: **Mental & Emotional Wellness**

**The invisible struggle:**

Mental health struggles often not visible. Seseorang bisa smile di rapat tapi struggling inside.

**Common mental health challenges di organisasi mahasiswa:**

1. **Anxiety tentang performance**
   - "Apakah kerja saya cukup baik?"
   - "Kalau saya gagal, akan mengecewakan semua orang"
   - Perfectionism yang melumpuhkan

2. **Imposter syndrome**
   - "Saya tidak deserve posisi ini"
   - "Orang lain lebih capable dari saya"
   - Constantly comparing self dengan others

3. **FOMO & overwhelm**
   - Takut ketinggalan kalau tidak attend semua events
   - Feeling guilty saat say no
   - Overcommitment karena tidak bisa refuse

4. **Conflict & relationship stress**
   - Interpersonal tension
   - Fear of confrontation ‚Üí masalah dipendam
   - Feeling excluded atau not valued

5. **Pressure dari multiple roles**
   - Juggling kuliah + organisasi + personal life + maybe kerja
   - Feeling like failing di semua areas
   - No time untuk self-care

**What organizations can do:**

**A. Normalize Mental Health Conversations**

```
SHIFT THE CULTURE:

‚ùå OLD STIGMA:
"Mental health itu weakness"
"Kalau kamu struggle, kamu tidak tough enough"
"Just push through, don't be soft"

‚úÖ NEW NORMAL:
"Mental health is health. Period."
"It's okay to not be okay. It's okay to ask for help."
"Taking care of mental health adalah strength, bukan weakness"
```

**Implementation:**

```
1. LEADERSHIP VULNERABILITY
   - Leaders openly share (appropriate level): "Saya juga pernah overwhelmed, ini yang saya lakukan..."
   - Model healthy coping: "This week crazy, jadi weekend saya full rest untuk recharge"
   - Normalize therapy/counseling: "Saya consult dengan counselor kampus, very helpful"

2. REGULAR CHECK-INS
   Structure check-ins yang beyond "How's work?"
   
   Questions:
   - "How are you REALLY doing?" (with genuine interest)
   - "What's been hard this week?"
   - "What's bringing you joy lately?"
   - "Is your workload manageable?"
   - "What support do you need?"

3. MENTAL HEALTH RESOURCES
   - Compile list of resources:
     * Campus counseling services (contact, hours, how to access)
     * Mental health hotlines (Into The Light, etc.)
     * Apps (Riliv, Halodoc untuk consult psikolog online)
     * Self-help resources (articles, videos)
   - Share in Wiki and periodically remind members

4. MENTAL HEALTH DAYS
   Policy: "Mental health day is valid reason untuk tidak hadir"
   - No need elaborate explanation
   - No guilt-tripping
   - "Take care of yourself" response, not "Tapi deadlinenya gimana?"
```

**B. Create Psychological Safety**

**Apa itu psychological safety?**

> Environment di mana people feel safe untuk take risks, speak up, make mistakes, ask for help, tanpa takut dihakimi, dipermalukan, atau dihukum.

**Why it matters:**

Dengan psychological safety:
- ‚úÖ Innovation meningkat (orang berani share crazy ideas)
- ‚úÖ Learning meningkat (orang admit mistakes dan belajar dari itu)
- ‚úÖ Engagement meningkat (orang feel safe untuk fully participate)
- ‚úÖ Well-being meningkat (tidak perlu constant pretending atau hiding)

Tanpa psychological safety:
- ‚ùå People play safe (no risk-taking)
- ‚ùå Mistakes disembunyikan (jadi tidak belajar)
- ‚ùå Silence di meetings (takut dihakimi)
- ‚ùå High stress & anxiety

**How to build psychological safety:**

```
1. LEADER BEHAVIORS

‚úì Admit your own mistakes openly
  "I made wrong call di X. Here's what I learned."

‚úì Ask for input genuinely
  "I don't have all answers. What do you think?" (and actually listen)

‚úì Respond well to bad news
  ‚ùå "How could this happen? Who's responsible?"
  ‚úÖ "Okay, this happened. What can we learn? How do we move forward?"

‚úì Thank people for speaking up
  "Thank you for raising this concern. This is exactly what we need to hear."

‚úì Frame failures as learning
  "This didn't work, but now we know. Let's try differently."

2. TEAM NORMS

‚úì "No stupid questions" culture
  - Explicitly state this in onboarding
  - Leaders model by asking basic questions

‚úì "Fail forward" mindset
  - Celebrate smart failures (where we learned something)
  - Post-mortem yang fokus pada lesson, bukan blame

‚úì Constructive disagreement welcomed
  - "Strong opinions, loosely held"
  - "Disagree and commit" culture
  - Separate ideas from identity (critique idea ‚â† attack person)

‚úì Help-seeking is normalized
  - "I need help with X" is respected, not seen as incompetence
  - Asking for help adalah sign of self-awareness

3. STRUCTURAL PRACTICES

‚úì Anonymous feedback channels
  - Google Forms untuk submit concerns anonymously
  - Suggestion box (digital atau physical)
  - Quarterly "Ask Me Anything" with anonymized questions

‚úì Regular pulse checks
  "How safe do you feel to speak up in this team?" (1-5 scale)
  Track over time, address drops

‚úì Clear consequences untuk toxic behavior
  - Zero tolerance untuk bullying, harassment, discrimination
  - Swift action when someone violates safety
```

**C. Stress Management Programs**

```
PROACTIVE STRESS SUPPORT:

1. WORKSHOPS & SESSIONS
   
   Topics yang relevant:
   - Time management & prioritization
   - Stress management techniques (breathing, grounding)
   - Mindfulness & meditation intro
   - Healthy boundaries & saying no
   - Dealing with perfectionism
   - Managing anxiety
   
   Format:
   - 60-90 menit interactive session
   - Facilitator: bisa counselor kampus, trained peer, atau external
   - Quarterly atau as-needed

2. MINDFULNESS MOMENTS
   
   Incorporate into routines:
   - Start meetings dengan 2 menit guided breathing
   - "Mindful Monday" reminder di group chat
   - Share tips & techniques

3. STRESS RELIEF ACTIVITIES
   
   Examples:
   - Coloring session (adult coloring books)
   - Board game night
   - Movie night
   - Art & craft session
   - Journaling workshop
   
   Purpose: Fun, low-stakes, no work talk

4. SUPPORT CIRCLES
   
   Small groups (4-6 orang) yang meet regularly just to check-in:
   - Vulnerable sharing (confidential)
   - Peer support
   - No pressure, pure support
```

**D. Boundaries & Saying No**

Mengajarkan dan mempermudah anggota untuk set boundaries.

```
ORGANIZATION'S ROLE IN BOUNDARIES:

1. EXPLICIT PERMISSION TO SAY NO

Policy statement di handbook:
"Anda berhak untuk decline tasks atau commitments yang beyond capacity Anda. 
Saying no is not letting the team down - it's being responsible dan honest. 
We value your well-being over any single task."

2. MODEL SAYING NO

Leaders demonstrate:
"I'd love to help, but my plate is full right now. Can we find someone else 
atau push the timeline?"

3. PROVIDE SCRIPTS

Help people say no gracefully:

"Thank you for thinking of me untuk X. Right now I'm committed to Y dan Z, 
dan I wouldn't be able to give X the attention it deserves. Can I suggest 
[alternative person] atau can we revisit this in [timeframe]?"

"I appreciate the opportunity. However, untuk maintain quality of my current 
responsibilities, I need to decline. Thank you for understanding."

4. RESPECT NOs

‚ùå "Come on, just this once. I'm sure you can squeeze it in."
‚ùå "Really? I thought you were committed to this organization."

‚úÖ "Thank you for being honest about your capacity. Let's figure out alternatives."
‚úÖ "I appreciate you setting boundaries. How can we support you?"

5. CAPACITY CHECKS

Before assigning tasks:
"On scale 1-10, how's your capacity right now?"
If 7+: "Okay, this might be too much. Let's figure out another way."
```

#### Dimensi 3: **Social & Community Wellness**

**Humans are social creatures.** Sense of belonging dan quality relationships directly impact well-being.

**The challenge:**

Organisasi bisa secara paradoks membuat orang feel:
- ‚ùå Isolated (everyone busy, no real connection)
- ‚ùå Competitive (unhealthy comparison, scarcity mindset)
- ‚ùå Excluded (cliques, in-groups vs out-groups)

**What healthy social wellness looks like:**

- ‚úÖ Sense of belonging: "This is my tribe"
- ‚úÖ Authentic connections: Relationships beyond work
- ‚úÖ Mutual support: "We got each other's backs"
- ‚úÖ Inclusion: Everyone feels welcomed dan valued
- ‚úÖ Celebration: Shared joy, not just shared work

**What organizations can do:**

**A. Intentional Bonding Time**

```
Jangan hanya work together, bond together.

1. MONTHLY SOCIAL HANGOUTS (Non-Work)

Characteristics:
- No work talk allowed
- Informal, relaxed setting
- Fun activities

Ideas:
- Karaoke night
- Bowling
- Hiking/nature trip
- Board game cafe
- Movie marathon
- Cooking/potluck together
- Volunteer together (community service)

Keys:
* Scheduled in calendar early (so people can commit)
* Budget allocated (organisasi sponsor or split cost fairly)
* Inclusive (consider different interests/accessibility)
* Optional tapi encouraged (not mandatory, but FOMO will make people come üòÑ)

2. COFFEE CHATS / RANDOM COFFEE

System: Random pairing untuk casual 1-on-1 chats

How:
- Monthly random pairing via tool atau manual
- 30-60 menit coffee/meal together
- Get to know each other beyond organizational roles
- Organizational budget covers coffee/snack (small investment, big impact)

3. CELEBRATION RITUALS

Celebrate:
- Birthdays (monthly birthday celebration untuk semua yang lahir bulan itu)
- Personal achievements (graduation, awards, internship secured)
- Organizational wins (event success, milestones)
- Cultural/religious holidays (inclusive untuk semua)

Format:
- Simple tapi sincere
- Public acknowledgment
- Small gifts/treats

4. WELCOMING RITUALS UNTUK NEW MEMBERS

First impression matters:

- Welcome package (organizational merch, handbook, welcome note)
- Buddy system (assigned mentor for first month)
- Welcome lunch/dinner (whole team)
- Personal introduction di group chat (with fun facts)
- Photo session untuk organizational profile
```

**B. Foster Inclusive Culture**

```
INCLUSION PRINCIPLES:

1. DIVERSITY IS VALUED

Appreciate differences:
- Different majors, backgrounds, interests
- Different working styles (introverts & extroverts)
- Different opinions dan perspectives

Explicitly state: "We're better because of our differences, not despite them."

2. NO CLIQUES

Red flags:
- Always same people lunch together, others excluded
- Inside jokes yang make others feel left out
- Information sharing yang selective
- Decision making by small group without involving others

Prevention:
- Rotate meeting facilitators
- Mix up teams for projects
- Inclusive language ("Hey everyone" bukan "Hey guys")
- Call out exclusive behavior gently but firmly

3. AMPLIFY QUIET VOICES

Systems:
- "Popcorn style" sharing (random call, not just who speaks up)
- Written brainstorming before verbal (so introverts can contribute)
- Explicitly invite: "A, we haven't heard from you. What's your thought?"
- Validate contributions: "That's great point, thank you for sharing"

4. ACCESSIBILITY CONSIDERATIONS

Think about:
- Physical accessibility (venue, seating)
- Financial accessibility (don't assume everyone can afford X)
- Time accessibility (respect class schedules, part-time jobs)
- Technology accessibility (not everyone has latest gadgets)

5. ZERO TOLERANCE UNTUK DISCRIMINATION

Clear policy:
- No discrimination based on: race, religion, gender, sexual orientation, 
  socioeconomic status, physical ability, atau any other identity
- Harassment of any kind is unacceptable
- Swift action when violations occur
```

**C. Conflict Resolution & Relationship Repair**

Kita sudah bahas di Bab 4 tentang conflict management. Dalam konteks wellness:

```
HEALTHY CONFLICT CULTURE:

‚úì Conflict addressed early (tidak dibiarkan fester)
‚úì Focus on behavior, not person
‚úì Both parties heard
‚úì Mediation available
‚úì Relationship repair prioritized (bukan just "move on")
‚úì Learning dari conflict

POST-CONFLICT CARE:

Setelah resolution:
1. Check-in dengan both parties individually
   "How are you feeling after conversation?"
   
2. Monitor relationship
   Are they able to work together produktif? Any lingering tension?
   
3. Team acknowledgment (kalau appropriate)
   "We had tough conversation, but we worked through it. That's growth."
   
4. Prevent escalation in future
   "What can we put in place so this type of issue addressed earlier next time?"
```

### 7.3 Implementasi: Building Wellness Culture Step-by-Step

Budaya tidak built overnight. Ini adalah iterative process.

#### PHASE 1: Assessment & Awareness (Month 1)

**A. Survey Current State**

```
WELLNESS PULSE SURVEY

Rate 1-5 (1=Strongly Disagree, 5=Strongly Agree):

PHYSICAL WELLNESS:
1. Organizational activities respect my sleep schedule
2. Meeting schedules are reasonable dan considerate
3. I feel physically healthy while active in this organization
4. I have time untuk exercise/physical activity

MENTAL & EMOTIONAL WELLNESS:
5. I feel my workload is manageable
6. I feel safe to express concerns atau struggles
7. I don't experience excessive stress from organizational activities
8. Leadership cares about my mental well-being

SOCIAL WELLNESS:
9. I feel sense of belonging in this organization
10. I have genuine friendships here beyond work relationships
11. I feel included dan valued
12. Conflicts are handled constructively

WORK-LIFE BALANCE:
13. I have adequate time untuk personal life
14. I don't feel guilty when prioritizing self-care
15. Organizational demands don't negatively impact my academics

OPEN-ENDED:
- What's one thing that would significantly improve your well-being in this org?
- Do you have any wellness concerns you'd like to share? (Anonymous)
```

**Analyze results:**
- Identify areas dengan lowest scores
- Common themes dari open-ended responses
- Priority areas untuk improvement

**B. Leadership Workshop**

Educate leaders first:

```
WELLNESS LEADERSHIP TRAINING (Half-day workshop)

Topics:
1. What is wellness culture dan why it matters
2. Signs of burnout (in self dan others)
3. How to have supportive conversations
4. Setting healthy boundaries dan modeling
5. Creating psychological safety
6. Resource awareness (where to refer people for help)

Outcome:
- Leaders equipped dengan skills
- Commitment to wellness as priority
```

#### PHASE 2: Policy & Systems (Month 2-3)

**Create Wellness Charter**

```
[NAMA ORGANISASI] WELLNESS CHARTER

OUR COMMITMENT:
We are committed to creating environment where every member can thrive 
physically, mentally, emotionally, dan socially.

PRINCIPLES:

1. WHOLE PERSON APPROACH
   We recognize members as whole people with lives beyond organization.
   Academic, family, personal well-being come first.

2. SUSTAINABLE PACE
   We value consistent, sustainable contribution over heroic burnout.
   Rest is productive. Boundaries are respected.

3. PSYCHOLOGICAL SAFETY
   We create space where people can speak up, make mistakes, ask for help,
   dan be authentic without fear.

4. INCLUSIVE COMMUNITY
   Everyone belongs here. Diversity is strength. Exclusion is not tolerated.

5. PROACTIVE SUPPORT
   We don't wait for crisis. We actively support well-being through systems,
   resources, dan culture.

POLICIES:

1. MEETING BOUNDARIES
   - No meetings after 21.00 unless emergency
   - Meetings max 90 minutes dengan break
   - Weekend meetings only when absolutely necessary

2. WORKLOAD MANAGEMENT
   - Right to decline tasks beyond capacity
   - Regular capacity check-ins
   - Redistribute work kalau someone overwhelmed

3. EXAM PERIOD SUPPORT
   - Organizational activities paused during midterms/finals
   - Flexible deadlines untuk academic priorities
   - No pressure to attend during exam weeks

4. MENTAL HEALTH DAYS
   - Mental health is valid reason untuk absence
   - No elaborate explanation required
   - No penalty atau guilt-tripping

5. OPEN DOOR POLICY
   - Any member can talk to any leader about well-being concerns
   - Confidentiality respected
   - Action taken when support needed

RESOURCES:

[List of mental health resources, contacts, how to access]

ACCOUNTABILITY:

- Quarterly wellness surveys untuk measure progress
- Regular check-ins dengan members
- Anonymous feedback channel
- Violations of wellness principles addressed seriously

SIGNED BY:

[All leadership team members]
[Date]

"We commit to upholding these principles dan holding each other accountable."
```

**Integrate into Operations**

Update existing SOPs dengan wellness lens:

```
Example: Meeting SOP Update

BEFORE:
"Meetings scheduled as needed to discuss tasks"

AFTER:
"Meetings scheduled with wellness considerations:
- Held between 18.00-21.00 on weekdays
- Max 90 minutes duration
- Agenda sent H-1
- Include 5-10 min break for meetings >60 min
- Snacks provided
- Start with quick mood check
- End with clear action items dan appreciation"
```

#### PHASE 4: Sustain & Iterate (Month 6+)

**A. Regular Measurement**

```

- Number of wellness program participants

Qualitative:
```

### 7.4 Handling Crisis: When Someone is Really Struggling

Despite best prevention, crises happen.

**Red Flags to Watch:**

```
SIGNS SOMEONE NEEDS IMMEDIATE SUPPORT:

üî¥ CRITICAL (Need immediate action):
- Suicidal ideation atau self-harm talk
- Severe depression (can't get out of bed, not eating)
- Panic attacks frequently
- Substance abuse
- Complete withdrawal (tidak respond, disappear)

üü° SERIOUS (Need attention soon):
- Persistent sadness atau hopelessness
- Significant behavior change
- Academic performance sudden drop
- Isolation dari friends
- Expressing overwhelming stress constantly
- Physical health deteriorating

üü¢ MONITOR (Early signs):
- Occasional stress comments
- Slightly decreased engagement
- Minor mood shifts
```

**Crisis Response Protocol:**

```
STEP 1: IMMEDIATE SAFETY

If imminent danger:
- DO NOT leave person alone
- Contact emergency services (119) or campus security
- Contact campus counseling center (have number ready)
- Inform parents/guardians (if appropriate dan with permission)
- Stay calm, be supportive

STEP 2: PRIVATE CONVERSATION
For serious but not immediate danger:

1. APPROACH WITH CARE
   "Hey, I've noticed [specific observations]. I'm concerned about you. 
   Can we talk?"
   
2. LISTEN WITHOUT JUDGMENT
   - Let them share
   - Don't minimize: "It's not that bad" ‚ùå
   - Don't fix immediately
   - Just listen dan validate

3. EXPRESS CONCERN
   "I care about you. I want to support you. You're not alone in this."

4. ASSESS NEED
   "Have you talked to anyone about this? Family? Counselor?"
   "What would be most helpful for you right now?"

5. OFFER RESOURCES
   - Campus counseling
   - Hotlines
   - Medical professional
   - Religious/spiritual support (if they're religious)
   
   "Would you be open to talking dengan counselor? I can help you make appointment."

6. ORGANIZATIONAL ACCOMMODATION
   "Let's remove organizational pressure for now. Your health is priority. 
   We'll figure out your responsibilities. Don't worry about that."

7. FOLLOW UP
   - Check in regularly (daily atau every few days)
   - "How are you doing? Did you connect with counselor?"
   - Continue support even after acute phase

STEP 3: INFORM APPROPRIATE PEOPLE

With person's permission:
- Inform core leadership (for accommodation purposes)
- May need to inform pembina/advisor
- Respect confidentiality (only need-to-know basis)

Without person's permission:
- Only if imminent danger to self atau others
- Duty to report overrides confidentiality in crisis

STEP 4: ORGANIZATIONAL RESPONSE

1. IMMEDIATE WORKLOAD RELIEF
   - Reassign responsibilities temporarily
   - No pressure untuk return
   - "Take all the time you need"

2. MAINTAIN CONNECTION (If they want)
   - Keep them in loop (non-work updates)
   - "You're still part of us. We're here when you're ready."
   - No isolation unless they prefer space

3. SMOOTH RETURN
   When they're ready:
   - Gradual reintegration
   - Flexible schedule
   - Check-ins
   - No stigma atau awkwardness

4. TEAM SUPPORT (Carefully)
   - Team may need know someone struggling (vague terms, no details)
   - "X is going through difficult time. Let's support by [specific ways]."
   - Respect privacy
```

**Leader Self-Care:**

Supporting someone in crisis is heavy. Leaders need care too.

```
AFTER CRISIS SUPPORT:

1. DEBRIEF
   - Talk dengan advisor/pembina
   - Process your emotions
   - "Did I handle that okay?"

2. SELF-CARE
   - You're not superhero
   - Seek support for yourself
   - Set boundaries (you can't be 24/7 counselor)

3. KNOW YOUR LIMITS
   - You're not trained therapist
   - Your role: connect to professional help, provide organizational support
   - Don't try to "fix" mental health issue

4. LEARN
   - Mental health first aid training (if available)
   - Resources about supporting others
```

Budaya kesejahteraan bukan luxury atau "nice-to-have". Ini adalah **fundamental requirement** untuk organisasi yang sustainable, productive, dan fulfilling.

**Key Takeaways:**

1. **Wellness is everyone's responsibility**, but leadership sets the tone
2. **Prevention is better than crisis management** - build systems sebelum ada masalah
3. **Small consistent actions** lebih powerful than big occasional gestures
4. **Culture change takes time** - be patient but persistent
5. **Measure what matters** - track wellness indicators as seriously as program metrics
6. **You can't pour from empty cup** - leaders must care for themselves to care for others

Organisasi yang prioritizes wellness akan:
- ‚úÖ Retain talented members
- ‚úÖ Achieve better results (rested people are effective people)
- ‚úÖ Build lasting community
- ‚úÖ Create positive ripple effects (members bring healthy practices to other areas of life)
- ‚úÖ Sustain across generations
